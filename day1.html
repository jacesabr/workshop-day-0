🔄 The Big Shift

📌 THE APP WE'RE BUILDING: A simple to-do list. You can add tasks and see them in a list.
❌ Old way (meaningless tech jargon):
"I need a MERN stack with MongoDB Atlas and JWT authentication using middleware and a RESTful API with CORS enabled."
✅ The AI Revolution way (just describe the screen):
What the app does: It's a to-do list. You can add tasks and see them in a list.

How it looks and behaves:
• The home page has a + Add Task button
• When I click it, a white box pops up with a text field and a green Save button
• I type something like "Buy milk" and click Save
• The white box disappears
• My new task "Buy milk" appears in the list below
• The newest task shows up at the top
🧠 What AI figures out by itself:
• It needs to remember each task (so it saves them somewhere)
• Each task belongs to whoever added it (so it knows who's who)
• When I click Save, something happens on the screen (it updates the list)
• Newest on top means it sorts them by date

You never said "database" or "backend" or "API endpoint." You just described a to-do list. The AI understands.

📢 The One Rule of the AI Revolution

Don't say technical terms that have minimal to no meaning in the AI revolution.

❌ "database" ❌ "API" ❌ "modal" ❌ "backend" ❌ "endpoint" ❌ "middleware" ❌ "authentication" ❌ "schema"
✅ "a white box pops up" ✅ "when I click Save, it appears in the list" ✅ "a text field where I type" ✅ "a green button that says Save" ✅ "the newest one shows at the top" ✅ "log in with Google"

Say what the user sees and what happens when they click. Nothing else matters.

2 📝 Describe what happens

Use normal human words — what the user sees, what happens when they click.

📌 EXAMPLE: Habit Tracker App
What the app does: You check off habits each day (like "exercise" or "read").

Describe it (no tech words):
  • The main screen shows a list of my habits with a checkbox next to each one
  • At the top, it shows today's date
  • There's a "+ New Habit" button at the bottom
  • When I click "+ New Habit", a small box slides up from the bottom with a text field where I type the habit name and a blue "Create" button
  • When I click Create, the box closes and my new habit appears in the list
  • When I check a checkbox, it turns green and that habit is marked "done" for today
  • Tomorrow, all the checkboxes reset to unchecked
🎯 Template: Describe your pages
"My app has: • A login screen with 'Sign in with Google' button • A main screen showing a list of my [whatever] • A settings screen where I can pick dark or light mode"
🔘 Template: Describe what buttons do
"When I click the + Add button: → A white box appears in the middle of the screen → There's a text field where I type [something] → There's a green 'Save' button → When I click Save, the white box disappears and my new [thing] appears in the list"

5 ⚡ Cursor builds the app

Now Cursor does the work. Your job is to watch, test, and approve — not to write code.

1
Open Cursor on your computer.
2
Paste Claude's plan into the Cursor chat panel (right side) and say: "Build this app step by step."
3
Watch Cursor write code. Files appear, code fills in. Don't read it — just watch. When it pauses and asks "Accept?", click Accept.

Test the app. Cursor will tell you the address (something like localhost:3000). Open it. Click around. If something looks wrong, just tell Cursor — "the Add button doesn't work, fix it."

Repeat: describe → fix → test → describe → fix → test. The whole app comes together this way.

No prompt engineering, no follow-up questions — layered analysis from a single input.

⏱️ The 15-Minute Validation Sprint

Phase 1 (5 min): Run your idea through ValidatorAI → "build / pivot / kill" verdict. Then Sturppy to model whether the unit economics work.

Phase 2 (10 min): DimeADozen.ai for a 60-second competitor + market report straight from your idea. Then ProjectMaven for a real effort estimate before you write a line of code.

Final step: Ask Claude: "Based on all four reports above, give me a one-sentence verdict: build this, pivot, or abandon?"
