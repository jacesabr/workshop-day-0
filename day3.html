Day 3 · Project Demo

Read any philosophy book. Master it through dialogue.

A Socratic AI tutor that reads the book with you, tests your understanding through conversation, and breaks down what you don't get — fractally, until you do.

The Problem

You read 30 pages of Nietzsche. Two days later, you can't explain a single concept.

Passive reading creates the illusion of understanding. Your eyes pass over the words, the prose feels profound, you nod along. But real comprehension is something else entirely — it's the ability to be asked something you can't bullshit through.

Socrates figured this out 2,400 years ago. You learn by being questioned. By having to explain. By being caught contradicting yourself and then having to reconcile it.

Until now, that meant hiring a tutor — one who'd read the same book, one who knows when you're faking it. Most people will never have that.

"You don't understand a thing until you can answer questions about it you weren't expecting."
The Solution

The tutor is now inside the book.

Upload a PDF of any philosophy text. The AI reads it, builds a map of every concept it contains, and starts a conversation with you about it.

You don't quiz yourself with flashcards. You don't underline and re-read. You talk — and the AI talks back, Socratically, asking the question that finds the exact spot where you don't actually understand.

How It Works

Four moves. Then it loops until you've mastered the book.

1

Drop in your PDF

The AI ingests the text, identifies every distinct concept (e.g. "the eternal recurrence", "amor fati", "the death of God"), and arranges them into a concept map — a tree showing which ideas depend on which.

2

Begin the dialogue

The AI doesn't lecture. It asks you a Socratic question about the first concept and listens to how you answer. Are you confident? Do you contradict the text? Are you parroting words you don't actually grasp?

3

Track confidence, per concept

Every exchange updates a confidence score for that concept — green at 85%+, yellow at 50-85%, red below. The map glows accordingly. You can see, at a glance, what you actually know vs what you only think you know.

4

Fractal deep-dive on the weak spots

When a concept is red or yellow, the AI doesn't repeat the explanation louder. It breaks the concept down into its prerequisites, researches them, tests those next. If those are also shaky, it goes deeper. Once the leaves are mastered, it climbs back up the tree — and now the parent concept clicks.

The Fractal Deep-Dive

When you're shaky, the concept breaks itself open.

Repeating an explanation louder doesn't help. The AI's move is different: it decomposes the misunderstood concept into its prerequisites and tests those first. If the prerequisites are also shaky, it goes deeper. The tree expands until every leaf is mastered. Then it walks back up the tree — and now the parent concept makes sense, because the foundations under it are solid.

Socratic Tutor
You said the Eternal Recurrence is "Nietzsche's idea that time repeats forever." Fair summary. But here's the harder question: does Nietzsche think this is literally true, or is it a thought experiment? What's the textual evidence either way?
I think it's literal? Because he keeps saying it in dramatic terms.
Socratic Tutor
Possible. But look at §341 of The Gay Science, where he frames it as a demon's offer — "would you not throw yourself down and gnash your teeth?" That's not a cosmological claim. It's a test of how you're living right now. So which is it? Both? Neither?
Hmm. I'm not sure.
Socratic Tutor
Good — that "I'm not sure" is the honest answer, and it's why I'm dropping your confidence on this. Let's go back: what does Nietzsche mean by amor fati, and how does it relate?

Under the Hood

The tech stack — simple, mostly free.

Gemini 2.5 Pro The dialogue brain. Long context window lets it hold the whole book + your conversation. Free tier on Google AI Studio.
PDF parsing Cursor wires in pdf-parse or Gemini's native PDF understanding to extract text + figure structure.
Concept extraction One Gemini call: "List every distinct philosophical concept in this text, with prerequisites." Gives us the map nodes & edges.
Confidence model After each user response, Gemini scores 0–100 on whether the answer was confident, accurate, original. Rolling average per concept.
Live mind map D3.js or React Flow renders the concept tree. Node colors bind to the confidence scores in real time.
Storage Supabase saves your conversations + confidence history. Pick the book back up next week, your map is where you left it.
Why Philosophy First

Because dialogue is its native medium.

Math and the hard sciences are conversation-resistant. You can't really Socratically question someone about L'Hôpital's rule — either they can apply it or they can't. The answer space is too narrow, the prerequisites too rigid.

Philosophy is the opposite. The text is the argument. Understanding means being able to reconstruct the argument, push back on it, see where it depends on prior assumptions, notice where Nietzsche contradicts himself between sections. That's exactly what a language model can do, well.

Once we prove the loop — map → dialogue → confidence → fractal deep-dive → mastery — on philosophy, the same engine lifts to history, theology, literature, law, then eventually to softer regions of the sciences (biology > physics > math, roughly).
