01 · The problem

Parents pay ₹2 lakh a year for a class of 40.

Coaching centres are a 30-year-old model: one teacher, one pace, one answer key. The kid who's stuck on chapter 4 sits through chapter 12 anyway. The kid who needs more practice on optics gets the same homework as everyone else.

India spends $58 billion a year on test prep. The success rate for JEE? Under 2%.

02 · What students actually need

A coach who knows them.

  • Knows which chapter they're weak in — not "Physics", but rotational mechanics, week 3.
  • Gives them their own question set, not the same worksheet as 40 other kids.
  • Explains the wrong answer until they actually get it — in their own language.
  • Never gets tired. Never has 39 other students. Never charges ₹2 lakh.
03 · Why this wasn't possible before

A private tutor used to mean a private tutor.

Until 2024, "personalised coaching" meant hiring a human. Hours, ego, scheduling, salary.

As of 2026, AI is good enough and cheap enough to give every Indian student their own tutor — for less than a Domino's pizza per month. This is the first time in history this has been possible.
